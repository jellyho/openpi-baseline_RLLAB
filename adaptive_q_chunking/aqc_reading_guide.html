§1 · The big picture

QC trains a fixed-size chunked critic and always executes H actions before replanning. AQC learns a critic that scores every prefix of a chunk at once and lets the execution horizon become state-dependent.

Action chunking gives multi-step value backups without the off-policy bias of n-step returns, because the critic conditions on the exact executed sequence (paper §3, Eq. 2). But a single fixed H forces one global trade-off between reactivity (short chunks, replan often) and temporal coherence (long chunks, fast backups). The optimal trade-off is state-dependent — long on a straight route, short near a turn (paper Fig. 1).

ACSAC makes the horizon adaptive with three moving parts, all of which exist in our code:

§2 · Concept primer

Six ideas to hold in your head before reading any file.

2.1 · Action chunk & prefix

A length-H chunk is a_{t:t+H} = (a_t,…,a_{t+H-1}). Its length-h prefix is the first h actions a_{t:t+h}. AQC reasons about all prefixes of one chunk (paper §4, first paragraph).

2.2 · Prefix-conditioned Q & cross-horizon comparability

Q(s, a_{t:t+h}) is the value of executing exactly h actions open-loop, then following the policy. ACSAC defines it as Q(s,a_{1:h}) = r^h_t + γ^h V(s_{t+h}) (paper Eq. 14) — so for every h it is "total discounted return from s_t". Same unit ⇒ the values are comparable across h, which is what makes a joint arg-max over prefix lengths meaningful (paper Thm G.9).

서로 다른 prefix 길이 h의 Q값들이 모두 "s_t에서의 총 할인 누적 보상"이라는 같은 단위를 갖기 때문에 직접 비교(arg-max)가 가능합니다. 이게 ACSAC의 핵심 전제입니다.

2.3 · Prefix consistency (why a Transformer, not an MLP)

The h-th output must depend only on (s, a_{1:h}) and not on the future actions a_{h:H}. A causal mask guarantees this by construction (paper Prop G.7). An MLP fed a truncated chunk would leak the suffix and is not guaranteed consistent (paper Remark G.8). We verified our critic is prefix-consistent to bit-exactness (§6 #1).

2.4 · Expected-prefix-max bootstrap (a generalized EMaQ)

The bootstrap value at a next state samples N chunks from the BC policy and maxes over all N×H prefixes: V*(s') = max_{n,k} min_K Q(s', ã^(n)_{1:k}) (paper Eq. 19). With H=1 this is exactly EMaQ. This operator is a γ-contraction whose fixed point is the adaptive policy's value (paper Thm G.13/G.15).

2.5 · Rejection sampling extraction (best-of-N over two axes)

The policy is the frozen flow-BC policy filtered by the critic. QC does best-of-N over candidate chunks; AQC does best-of-N×H over (candidate, prefix length) and executes the winning prefix (paper Eq. 8, Fig. 2). No behavior-reg coefficient to tune.

2.6 · Gradient-level loss averaging

The H per-horizon squared errors are averaged at the gradient level (one loss = mean over h), not by averaging the targets. This reduces variance while preserving sparse-reward signal in each target (paper §G.5, Remark G.20).

"Average the losses, not the targets." Averaging targets would collapse the per-horizon signals and dilute sparse rewards. Our loss does the right thing at aqc.py:123 — a single .mean() over the (K,B,H) error tensor.

agents/aqc.py critic_loss():98 — regress every prefix to its own h-step target, average the H squared errors.

Spot-check three things: (1) the index alignment — prefix h uses rewards[:,h-1], masks[:,h-1], γ^h, and bootstraps at next_obs[:,h-1]=s_{t+h}; (2) the target is stop_gradient'd (:118); (3) the loss is .mean() over all of (K,B,H) — that 1/H is the gradient-level averaging of §G.5.

Target network: the one deviation worth your attention

App B says ACSAC bootstraps from the online critic with the gradient stopped — no Polyak target network — relying on LayerNorm (SEEM) for stability. We default to that: use_target_critic=False (aqc.py :411), so _expected_prefix_max queries the 'critic' module with stored params and the whole target is stop_gradient'd (aqc.py:118). A Polyak target (τ=5e-3) is available as a fallback flag.

DD-1 (the design decision to scrutinize). The paper's value learning uses online-critic-as-target. We made that the default for faithfulness, but it relies on the Transformer's Pre-LN keeping the NTK bounded. If you ever see Q diverge, flip --agent.use_target_critic=True. Table 2 lists τ "(when used)" — it is for the target-network baselines, not ACSAC's main method.
Implements paper Eq. 19 + App B. Spot-check: (1) min over K happens before the max over (n,h) (clipped-double-Q on the bootstrap); (2) the reshape (M·N,H)→(M,N·H) groups the right candidates per state (proposals are repeat'd contiguously at :88); (3) default is the online-critic target (DD-1).
다음 상태마다 flow-BC로 N개의 chunk를 뽑아 (n,k) 전부에 대해 Q를 평가하고(앙상블은 먼저 min) 최댓값을 V*로 씁니다(EMaQ의 확장, Eq. 19). 기본값은 별도 target network 없이 온라인 critic + stop_gradient (App B). 불안정하면 use_target_critic=True로 Polyak target 사용.

Adaptive extraction ★ code

agents/aqc.py sample_actions():214 — the joint (n,h) arg-max that turns the chunk size state-dependent.

jit + variable-length output. A jit-compiled function can't return a data-dependent shape, so sample_actions returns the full best chunk (H·d_a,) plus a scalar h*.
